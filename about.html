Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is diverse and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Stocking Up on Supplies

Posted July 6, 2025

Whether you need sunscreen, bottled water, or some late-night snacks, Taniti has you covered. The island offers two full-size supermarkets and two smaller grocery stores. For night owls or last-minute needs, there's a 24-hour convenience store, perfect for grabbing sunscreen before your morning hike or snacks for your beach day.

Tip: Since Taniti uses the U.S. dollar and most shops accept major credit cards (plus euros and yen), you won’t need to worry about exchanging too much cash up front.

How to Travel Like a Local

Posted July 6, 2025

Taniti is easy to explore no matter your style of travel. If you're staying in Taniti City, public buses run daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. and the city itself is very walkable — especially the Merriton Landing area.

Want more freedom? Rental cars are available near the airport, and bicycles with helmets can be rented throughout the city (helmets are required by law). For broader island access, private buses and taxis also operate and are fairly easy to use.

Taniti Nightlife: Quiet Evenings or Dancing

Posted July 6, 2025

Taniti might be known for its beaches, but its nightlife is quietly blossoming. You’ll find several pubs, a local microbrewery, and a new dance club that stays lively until midnight — the legal cutoff for alcohol sales. Movie lovers, arcade players, and art enthusiasts will also find options around Merriton Landing.

Tip: The drinking age is 18, and although the law isn't strictly enforced, alcohol can't be sold or served from midnight to 9 a.m.
